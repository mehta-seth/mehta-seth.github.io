> The shopkeeper, the customer, and the rupee note are the three essential nouns of an Indian high street — everything else is verb.

When measured in transactions per minute, the average kirana store outpaces a small bank branch. Try to verify this with `count(transactions, per='minute')` and the data is more interesting than tidy.

### A finer point

The argument splits into three parts:

1. The shopkeeper as a **clearing house** — credit, change, mediator.
2. The shopkeeper as an *informal banker* — keeping a running tab.
3. The shopkeeper as a [community node](#) — the smallest unit of trust.

Each role compounds the others. None can be removed without breaking the whole.

## A second section heading

There are days, walking through a market in Kolhapur or Pune, when you can almost watch this in motion. Some quick examples:

- A grandmother buys eggs and pays next week.
- A delivery boy picks up rice on his employer's tab.
- A child is sent for half a kilo of sugar with no money at all.

Every one of these is a transaction. Every one is also a relationship. The accounting and the social fabric are the same thing<Footnote id="2" />.
